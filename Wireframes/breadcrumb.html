Breadcrumbs are a textual representation of a site’s structure, regardless of how users navigate. It provides information to users as to where they are located within the site and offers them shortcut links back to previously viewed pages, without using the back button or other navigation bars.

 

Breadcrumbs communicate to users where they are, relative to the rest of the application or site and are about context, not history.

WHAT IS IT FOR?

BREADCRUMB

IHTSDO

INTERACTION

Each link is added sequentially to reflect a user's path through a service. IMPORTANT: Links are not continually added for a user's entire browsing experience. They are merely reflections of the user's location within a site/service structure.

 

On-click of each step will link the user to that page accordingly.

BEHAVIOUR

Breadcrumbs should appear left aligned to the page content, near the top of the screen and beneath the masthead. They should not dominate the screen. Use when the structure of the service follows a strict hierarchical structure.
