Notifications are used as informational feedback to a user in response to an action. This could be an acknowledgement of an action, to inform them of an error as part of a form submission or as a status update.

WHAT ARE THEY FOR?

TOP NOTIFICATION BAR

IHTSDO

INTERACTION

 

Confirmation

An acknowledgement of a positive user action. No further action is required.

 

Errors

On submitting a form with incomplete or incorrect information, an error notification will display. It is identified by its error state design. The error message indicates that further action is required by the user.

 

News / Updates

Used as a means to inform the user of any important updates to the service or UI. This should be a one time occurrence.

BEHAVIOUR

Sometimes referred to as a 'page toast', notifications sit as a horizontal bar format at the top of the page, beneath the masthead (and breadcrumb if there is one) and push all page content down (never obscure existing page content). They should remain in position, in-page for the duration of the currently viewed page. The notification does not persist on linking to other pages.

 

The notification has a 'close' function to dismiss it. On-click, the notification is removed with a smooth fade out animation and in response, all page content moves up to fill the space left.
