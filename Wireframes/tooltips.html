A tooltip's primary purpose is to provide more information about a particular field or UI element, should the label not be enough. They allow a user to retrieve that content on demand, without it unnecessarily cluttering the page.

WHAT ARE THEY FOR?

TOOLTIPS

IHTSDO

INTERACTION

 

On-click of related icon, the tooltip is invoked and its content displayed, by sitting on top of the currently viewed page. Users can close the tooltip on-click of the same icon or by clicking anywhere else on the page. If required, any content within the tooltip can be interacted with (copying text, performing action).

 

Tooltip size is variable and their behaviour liquid. The tooltip can be any width to accommodate content as required, but be mindful to keep this within reasonable levels. The tooltip is a support device and should fulfil its role as succinctly and efficiently as possible.

BEHAVIOUR

Tooltips are used in instances when a user may require further information to understand a task or term. They can also be used to present an action such as copying text or a URL.
